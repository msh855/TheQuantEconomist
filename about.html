About

Welcome to my blog!

The only important information you really need to know about me is that I grew up in the beautiful Meteora.

Other than this, my boring bio accumulates roughly 10 years of experience as a macroeconomist, mainly working in policy-making, economic research, and academia. Please have a look at my Linkedin profile if you are interested in my “CV”.

Academically, I specialize in Fiscal Policy, Macroeconomic Theory, and Computational Economics. I have worked in the applications of General Equilibrium with Incomplete Asset Markets, and as an economist I am strongly influenced by the works of Peter Diamond, T. Sargent, and O. Blanchard. The combined influences of their work on my research helped me to build a solid knowledge around fiscal policy, public debt, and macroeconomic and finance theory.

Computational Economics came as a byproduct of my research. I ended up discovering that I enjoy coding, programming and got reasonably good at it. I developed own ways of solving stochastic macroeconomic models with large state-space. Because of this strand of research, I later on discovered Machine Learning as it was related to some of the stuff I was working (See here for what I mean).

Since I cannot separate my quant and economist side, I decided to name this blog “The Quant Economist”, but I mainly aim to write (infrequently) about macroeconomic and finance related topics, and applied data science material. I believe that evidence based economics is pretty much data science, and data science is a good tool for better economics. Not to mention that data science (or Machine Learning) is the new econometrics. So, whenever I post about some applied data science material this is mostly to promote some good use-cases in macroeconomics or finance.

Ah, one final plead. I encourage you to call me Safis. This is my nickname name and how my family and friends call me.

I hope you enjoy the content here.
