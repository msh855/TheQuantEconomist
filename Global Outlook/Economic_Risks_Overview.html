Economic Risks Overview by Region

This document provides an overview of potential downside and upside risks for different countries and regions based on Oct 2024, FM, WEO, GFSR discussions and economic data and projections by IMF.

Europe

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks Deeper or more prolonged contraction in economies, particularly those reliant on external financing. Increased investment could enhance supply-side capacity and mitigate inflationary pressures.
Tighter-than-necessary monetary policy could further dampen growth. Public investment in EU countries could contribute to a higher-than-projected recovery in global demand.
Persistent inflation could erode consumer purchasing power and dampen demand.
Fiscal Risks Elevated public debt levels in many countries could limit fiscal space to respond to shocks.
Social & Political Risks Rising cost of living and energy prices could fuel social unrest. Successful integration of Ukrainian refugees into the European labour market could boost the labour supply and growth.
Political instability and fragmentation could hinder policy coordination and reform efforts.

US

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks The sources do not explicitly mention downside risks for the US economy. Stronger recovery in investment could contribute to a higher-than-projected recovery in global demand.

China

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks Deeper or more prolonged contraction in the property sector could have adverse spillover effects on the global economy. Reforms to strengthen the social safety net, leading to a decrease in the private saving rate, could boost global demand.
China’s shift towards a more consumption-driven economy could dampen demand for commodities.
Financial Market Risks Financial vulnerabilities, including high corporate debt levels and shadow banking activities, could pose risks to financial stability and economic growth.

Asia (excluding China)

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks Emerging market and developing economies in the region reliant on external financing are vulnerable to tighter global financial conditions. Surging demand for semiconductors and electronics driven by significant investment in artificial intelligence could fuel growth.
Fiscal Risks Rising debt levels in some countries could constrain fiscal policy space.

Commodity Countries

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks Declining oil prices could pose challenges for oil exporters.
China’s shift towards a more consumption-driven economy could dampen demand for commodities.

Emerging Countries

Risk Category Downside Risks Upside Risks
Macroeconomic Risks Emerging market and developing economies heavily reliant on external financing are vulnerable to tighter global financial conditions and could face debt sustainability challenges. Resilience of emerging markets, particularly those with strong fundamentals and proactive policy responses, could drive growth.
Shocks like conflicts, civil unrest, and disruptions to commodity production and shipping could lead to downward revisions in growth outlooks.
Financial Market Risks Increased synchronisation of monetary policy cycles with those in advanced economies could amplify the impact of interest rate changes in advanced economies.
Fiscal Risks Relatively loose fiscal policy stances on average in emerging markets could contribute to rising debt levels. Fiscal consolidation in developing economies could enhance debt sustainability and create space for future stimulus measures.
